# UFLI Audio Companion MVP Test Facilitator Script

## Goal
Test whether the UFLI audio companion improves access, persistence, and support for children ages 5-8 with ADHD traits without replacing decoding instruction.

## Scope
- Lessons: `1`, `14`, `95`
- Use one approved pilot voice only
- Audio is a companion artifact, not the primary teacher
- Compare:
  - `No TTS`: normal UFLI-aligned support without audio companion
  - `TTS Companion`: same lesson plus approved audio clips

## Required Clip Use Rules
- Allowed support clips:
  - `lesson_instruction`
  - `phoneme_model`
  - `word_model`
  - `review`
- Passage clips:
  - `passage_sentence`
  - `passage_full`
- Passage clips may be played only after the child attempts decoding first.
- Do not use audio to reveal answers before the child has attempted the task.
- Limit replay to `2` plays per clip unless the child is dysregulated and the facilitator documents why more support was needed.

## Session Design
- Each child completes 2 short sessions on matched material:
  - Session A: `No TTS`
  - Session B: `TTS Companion`
- Counterbalance order:
  - Half of children start with `No TTS`
  - Half start with `TTS Companion`
- Keep facilitator prompts, timing, and lesson targets as similar as possible across both sessions.

## Materials Needed
- Printed worksheet or lesson support sheet
- Audio playback device and headphones or speaker
- Child score sheet
- Adult observation rubric
- Summary template

## Before the Child Arrives
1. Confirm the lesson target and target words.
2. Confirm the session condition: `No TTS` or `TTS Companion`.
3. Open the correct review packet and audio playlist if the session uses TTS.
4. Review the clip order before starting.

## Opening Script
Say:

> We are going to practice reading. I want to see what helps you stay with the reading job. If something feels tricky, that is okay. We will try one step at a time.

If the session is `TTS Companion`, say:

> Sometimes you will hear a short helper voice. The helper voice is there to support you, but you still do the reading work.

## During the Session
1. Start with the worksheet or lesson prompt.
2. Ask the child to attempt the target word or sentence first.
3. In `TTS Companion`, play the matching support clip only after the child attempts the item or when the protocol specifically allows it.
4. Record:
   - decoding accuracy
   - number of redirects/prompts
   - replays used
   - whether the child completed the task
   - visible frustration or regulation issues
5. Keep prompts neutral and brief.

## Allowed Facilitator Prompts
- "Try the first sound."
- "Tap the sounds."
- "Read it slowly."
- "Point to the next word."

## Not Allowed
- Giving the whole word before the child attempts it
- Turning passage audio into the first reading exposure
- Praising speed over careful decoding
- Letting the child loop audio repeatedly instead of reading

## End of Session
Say:

> Thank you. You finished the reading practice. Now I am going to write down what seemed helpful.

Then complete:
- child score sheet
- adult observation rubric
- session summary template

## Minimum Success Signal
The TTS companion is promising only if it:
- does not reduce decoding accuracy
- reduces adult redirects/prompts
- improves completion or time on task
- does not create clear audio dependence
- remains calm and low stimulation
